Intro¶

Increasing revenue is essential to any business. It becomes exponentially easier to increase revenue when you know the habits of your customer base and can group them so you can have custom marketing and other aspects of business for each customer. This both improves the customer experience, cuts down on inefficent spending, and increases revenue. That is why I decided to use a mall customer dataset. I want to cluster the customers based off of their spending habits and demographics so I can then provide them with improved experiences and increase revenue for the business.

What is Clustering?¶

Clustering is an unsupervised learning algorithm that uses unlabeled data and groups together similar data points based off of their features. The algorithm looks at the data and finds patterns and puts these data points into clusters based on these similarities. K-Means the one that I will be using works by randomly initializing a certain number of clusters around random centroids. You then assign each data point to the nearest center and then find the mean position of all the points and then assign the new centroid. You then continue this process until the change becomes very infinitesimal

Data¶

We will be using a mall customer segmentation dataset that I found off of kaggle. It has plenty of features that will help me derive data driven insights to improve the business. Some of these include demographic information such as age or gender. We also have annual income and spending score.

Clustering¶

Why are we using K-Means specifically over any other algorithms? Well because of some aspects of the dataset k-means is actually perfect for it. The features are all numeric and standardized so there is no need for any mixed type clustering or anything super complex. K-Means is also scaleable so it would work with a much larger dataset as well. But mostly K-Means solves the task at hand which is to cluster the customers. There is no need to introduce added complexity if it is not needed.

Storytelling¶

As we can see there are 5 clear clusters of customers. I've classified the clusters into 5 groups high earning high spending individuals, high earning high spending, low earning high spending, low earning low spending, and medium earning medium spending. I want to create marketing campaigns that target the customers that will bring in the most revenue. As we can see the medium spending and medium earning cluster appears to be the highest number but there are also quite a large amount of high earning high spending customers. Because those customers bring in a greater amount of revenue it makes sense to focus on them. We can bring them in on a loyalty rewards program and provide them with free amenities to keep them spending in the store as they are already high earning and high spending so coupons may not make the biggest impact on their spending but making them feel special will. As for the low earning and high spending cluster we could send them coupons to keep them spending more without completely wiping out their money as they do not have as much to spend. The medium earning and medium spending could get added onto an email newsletter where we inform them of current deals so we can keep them having the mall and products on their mind to encourage spending and keep those customers in our environment. As for the low spending customers it may not make sense to spend as much on marketing as they generally spend a lower amount so you would have to spend more on marketing to get them to buy more.

Impact¶

There are a lot of possible ethical implications of this process. We run the risk of treating customers as just numbers and treating them as nothing more than banks to be sucked dry of every last dollar. Also the high spending and low income cluster could really be impacted very badly if the mall took advantage of them by marketing specifically to them, even though they might not be able to spend as much. We could end up targeting those who have shopping addictions, thereby worsening their conditions, as this data does not account for that sort of thing. At the end of the day is it worth it to do all of this for the bottom line?
